Topic 1

Lung volumes & capacities

Physiology
At a glance
  • A capacity is two or more volumes added together.
  • Any capacity containing residual volume (RV, FRC, TLC) cannot be measured by spirometry.
  • FRC is the volume left after a normal expiration — where lung recoil and chest-wall recoil balance.
Physiology

Reading the spirometry trace

Four volumes stack into four capacities. The single trace below contains all of them — learn to read it and you've learned the whole topic.

  • TV — tidal volume, a normal quiet breath (~500 mL).
  • IRV / ERV — extra volume you can force in / out beyond a tidal breath.
  • RV — air that always remains; keeps alveoli open.
  • VC = IRV + TV + ERV · TLC = VC + RV · FRC = ERV + RV.
High-yield

Spirometry can't measure RV — so it can't give you FRC or TLC either. These need helium dilution or body plethysmography.

Obstructive vs restrictive disease

Pathology

One ratio splits the whole field

The FEV₁/FVC ratio is the master switch. Trouble getting air out lowers it; trouble getting air in preserves or raises it.

Physiology

V/Q matching

Ideal gas exchange needs ventilation (V) matched to perfusion (Q). V/Q → 0 is a shunt (perfused but not ventilated, e.g. airway obstruction); V/Q → ∞ is dead space (ventilated but not perfused, e.g. pulmonary embolism).

Clinical correlation

Hypoxemia from a true shunt barely improves with 100% O₂; hypoxemia from V/Q mismatch does. That response is a classic distinguishing question.

Topic 3

Lung development

Embryology
Embryology

Surfactant is the punchline

Lungs mature through stages; the prize is type II pneumocytes making surfactant from around week 24, peaking near term. Surfactant lowers alveolar surface tension and keeps small alveoli from collapsing.

  • Type I pneumocytes — thin cells for gas exchange (95% of surface).
  • Type II pneumocytes — make surfactant; the stem cell that regenerates the alveolus.
  • Club (Clara) cells — secretory, non-ciliated; also a reserve cell.
Don't confuse

Too little surfactant → neonatal respiratory distress syndrome in premature infants. A low lecithin : sphingomyelin < 1.5 ratio predicts it.
